A towering cultural figure in his own lifetime and after, Beethoven bridged the gap between the Classical and Romantic periods of Western art music. His compositions redefined traditional notions of what music was or could be, paving the way for new paradigms of emotional expression, formal innovation, and the societal concept of the artist as a whole.
